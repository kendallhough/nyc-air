New York City Air Quality by Neighborhood 2019

Fine Particulate Matter (PM2.5)

Air pollution, of which fine particulate matter is a major contributor, is an environemtnal threat to dense urban populations. These particles are emitted from vehicles, boilers, and other combustion of fossil fuels and affect human health (namely heart and lung functions). The map above was created to visualize how vulnerability varies across neighborhoods in New York City.

Legend

Air quality data used for this project was gathered from the NYC Environment & Health Data Portal. The mean PM.2 (mcg per cubic meter) from the year 2019 was extracted. Next, using QGIS 3.2 on macOS 12.0.01, the data was joined to a NYCOpenData shapefile containing neighborhood boundaries.

After preparing the data, it was uploaded to Mapbox and stylized into a satellite choropleth map using a stock Mapbox tilesets.

Images from Gotham Gazette and the CDC.
